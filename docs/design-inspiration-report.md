# Design Inspiration Report: Top Home-Service Brands for Jerry & Co. Home Improvement LLC

## TL;DR
- The strongest digital model for Jerry & Co. is the "design-forward, premium-but-approachable" archetype best embodied by **Block Renovation** (web/brand system), **ZK Painting** (a Boston/Cape Cod/RI painter with ~490K TikTok followers who proves the "high-gloss spray finish" niche is a content goldmine in Jerry & Co.'s exact geography), and **Sweeten** (~410K Instagram followers built entirely on before/after reno reveals).
- Across web, social, and paid media the winning pattern is identical: real (never stock) before/after photography shot at consistent angles, a restrained editorial palette (warm neutrals, off-white, one deep accent), generous white space, lead-safe/craft trust signals, and short-form transformation video as the primary social and paid engine.
- Jerry & Co. should retire the orange/black/white scheme for a quieter premium palette, build the website around filterable before/after galleries and dedicated per-service pages with visible "Get a Free Estimate" CTAs, and make factory-grade spray-finish transformation/ASMR reels its signature content format.

## Key Findings

Home services is an unusually visual category, and the brands that "punch above their weight" digitally nearly all do the same handful of things well. The research surfaced 13 companies worth studying, grouped by category. The most transferable lessons for an equity-rich Greater Boston audience come from premium remodel-tech brands (Block, Sweeten, Curbio), craft-positioned painters (ZK Painting, Nash Painting, Catchlight, Arch, Monk's), and cabinet-refinishing specialists (N-Hance, Rocket Finish, Mint).

A recurring theme: **the quality of the website and feed is read by homeowners as a proxy for the quality of the craftsmanship.** The exact "X% of homeowners check social before hiring" stats that circulate in contractor-marketing blogs are largely unverifiable, but the directionally reliable data is striking — BrightLocal's 2026 consumer survey found that 24% of consumers visit a business's social media channels during their research process; Clear Seas Research (via ACHR News) found 91% of respondents rate online reviews as an important factor when choosing a contractor; and Service Direct's homeowner survey found 59% of respondents are more likely to hire a contractor whose website has videos. For Jerry & Co.'s "quality over price" ICP, the digital presence is the single most important trust signal.

## Details

### Category 1 — Premium remodel/refresh tech brands (best web + brand systems)

**1. Block Renovation — blockrenovation.com (Instagram @blockrenovation, ~52K followers)**
- Services overlap: bathroom and kitchen renovation, design services; serves Greater Boston among its metros.
- Web design: the gold-standard reference. Minimalist UI, calming/warm color palette, generous white space, clean layout, and an interactive "Renovation Studio" tool that lets users visualize design choices and estimate costs. CTAs ("Contact," "View Work," "Get Started") are always front-and-center. Block has raised $104M+ in venture funding, including a $50M Series C led by SoftBank Vision Fund 2 (with NEA, Lerer Hippeau, Obvious Ventures, and designer Kelly Wearstler among backers), positioning itself as fixing "the $450 billion renovation industry." Co-founders are T. Luke Sherwin (co-founder of mattress brand Casper) and CEO Koda Wang (former Chief Customer Officer at Rent the Runway and COO of HuffPost) — consumer-brand DNA rather than contractor DNA, and it shows.
- Notable detail: Block commissioned playful CG/3D product visualizations (tools, materials, fixtures) from Volley Studio that became synonymous with the brand identity and were reused across social, print, and launch ads — a smart way to maintain a cohesive visual system without constantly re-shooting.
- Social: editorial/educational content (design-trend explainers like "cottagecore" material posts) rather than pure portfolio dumps.
- Design style tag: **"bright airy minimalist / editorial DTC."**
- Why relevant: the closest template for a "Claude Design" premium system — it proves a renovation brand can feel like a lifestyle brand. Directly transferable for the rebrand away from orange/black.

**2. Sweeten — sweeten.com (Instagram @sweeten_home, ~410K followers)**
- Services overlap: full renovation matchmaking incl. bathroom, kitchen, paint; lists Boston (BOS) as a market.
- Social: the standout. ~410K Instagram followers built on a relentless cadence of homeowner before/after renovation reveals. Bio: "Renovate to live, Sweeten to THRIVE." Founder Jean Brownhill is a Cooper Union-trained architect, and the curation reflects it.
- Design style tag: **"aspirational editorial."**
- Why relevant: proves before/after transformation content is the highest-ceiling organic format in this category. Jerry & Co. should mine this for feed structure and caption style.

**3. Curbio — curbio.com (Instagram @curbiohq, ~13K followers)**
- Services overlap: pre-sale repairs, updates, paint, kitchen/bath makeovers — overlaps directly with Jerry & Co.'s pre-sale-seller ICP.
- Brand: rebranded in Jan 2022 with a new visual identity, refined color palette, new fonts, and polished brand photography to "elevate Curbio's premium home improvement solution," centered on its proprietary tech/app. Its VP of Marketing has publicly argued brands "don't invest in marketing & brand early enough."
- Design style tag: **"clean proptech / trustworthy modern."**
- Why relevant: a direct analog for the pre-sale-seller and property-manager ICPs; the rebrand rationale (invest in brand early to cement category position) is exactly Jerry & Co.'s situation.

### Paid media / ad creative observations

- **Format hierarchy:** Across home-services Meta advertising, **before/after carousels and before/after video are repeatedly named the top-performing creative for painting/refinishing contractors specifically.** Static images still reportedly drive the majority (~60–70%) of Meta conversions, but video is favored by the algorithm for reach. Recommended practice: refresh creative every 3–6 weeks; design for silent viewing (Digiday reports as much as 85% of video views happen with the sound off, per multiple publishers); hook within the first 3 seconds; use a 4:5 vertical crop in feed.
- **Hooks:** three structures dominate — pain-point opener, authority/credibility claim ("Best of Boston 2024–2026," "Fine Paints of Europe Master Certified"), and visual pattern-interrupt (a dramatic close-up of spray/gloss). Authority and visual-interrupt hooks fit Jerry & Co.'s premium positioning best.
- **Budget split:** a 70/20/10 split (proven winners / iterative variations / experimental) is a common recommendation; lead-gen ads report ~2–3x ROAS and retargeting (DPA) ~3–5x.
- **Big-brand context:** private-equity-backed Five Star Painting and CertaPro reportedly spend heavily on paid search ($40,000+/month in major metros per one agency source) — Jerry & Co. cannot outbid them and should instead win on tightly geo-fenced, high-intent transactional keywords (e.g., "cabinet refinishing [town]," "interior painting free estimate [town]") plus organic before/after social. CertaPro's brand is built around the trademarked "Certainty Service System®" and the line "The Power of Pro" (its spot tells viewers that for someone with the "power of certainty," choose a CertaPro Painter); it bills itself as "North America's largest residential painting contractor."
- **Caveat:** the CPL/ROAS and ad-spend figures come largely from marketing agencies promoting their own services and from vendor blogs citing secondhand stats (AppsFlyer/Meta); treat as directional, not audited. Exact CertaPro/Five Star ad-spend numbers could not be independently verified (iSpot data is paywalled).

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Brand system ("Claude Design"), weeks 0–4.**
- Retire orange/black/white. Adopt a premium palette modeled on Block/Sweeten: warm off-white/bone base, a deep anchor (charcoal, ink navy, or forest), one refined metallic or accent, and lots of white space. Pair a high-contrast modern sans for UI with an elegant serif for headlines (the "legacy luxury" web references in this space lean on Garamond-style serifs with black/off-white palettes to signal premium).
- Codify a before/after photo standard: identical angle/lighting, no stock, every job shot. This single discipline drives web, social, and paid simultaneously.
- Adopt a named, numbered process (à la Mint's 9-step) and a warranty/guarantee architecture (à la Nash's 10-year, Arch's White Glove, Catchlight's 3-year) plus lead-safe/EPA badges front and center.

**Stage 2 — Website rebuild, weeks 2–8.**
- Structure: Home, Services (separate pages for bathroom reno, gut-free refresh, spray cabinet refinishing, kitchen refresh, interior painting, exterior painting, tile), filterable before/after Gallery, About, Reviews, Contact.
- Borrow Block's interactive/estimate-forward UX, Rocket Finish's designer-color selector on the cabinet page, and Monk's per-project narrative pages for local SEO. Put a visible "Get a Free Estimate" CTA and click-to-call on every page; target <3-second load. Given that 59% of homeowners are more likely to hire a contractor whose site has video (Service Direct), embed your best transformation reels directly on service pages.

**Stage 3 — Social system, ongoing.**
- Make factory-grade spray-finish transformation/ASMR reels the signature format (the ZK Painting playbook, executed in a more premium register). Post 3–4x/week: a before/after reveal, a process/education clip, a color or "which-do-you-prefer" poll, and a testimonial/booking-update.
- Build cohesive branded templates (Block's CG-asset consistency is the model) so the feed reads as one system.

**Stage 4 — Paid media, after 8+ before/after assets exist.**
- Run geo-fenced, high-intent Google search on transactional cabinet/painting/bath keywords; avoid bidding wars with national franchises.
- On Meta, lead with before/after video + carousel, authority/visual-interrupt hooks, 4:5 vertical, silent-friendly captions; refresh every 3–6 weeks.

**Benchmarks that would change the plan:**
- If organic reels consistently clear ~10K+ views and generate inbound DMs, shift budget from paid search toward content production (the ZK/Idaho Painter model).
- If Meta CPL holds under ~$40 and ROAS >3x after 60 days, scale; if not, reallocate to local SEO and Google Local Services Ads.
- If the premium palette underperforms on quote-request rate vs. the old high-contrast scheme in an A/B test, revisit accent contrast rather than scrapping the whole system.
