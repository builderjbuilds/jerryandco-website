# Website Architecture & Page Strategy for Jerry & Co. Home Improvement LLC: A Competitor-Informed Gap Analysis and Build Plan

## TL;DR
- Across the seven competitor sites, the universal page architecture is: a **dedicated FAQ page**, a **"refinishing vs. replacement" comparison/cost page**, a **process/"how it works" page**, an **interactive visualizer tool**, a **before/after gallery as its own page**, **per-city service-area pages**, and an **About/brand-story page** — Jerry & Co.'s single-page site is missing all of these as standalone, indexable URLs, which is its single biggest competitive and SEO gap.
- The right move for a solo operator is NOT to copy the franchise model wholesale: replace the 15-year manufacturer-backed warranty with a **named, written workmanship guarantee plus a free touch-up bottle/visit**, replace "0% financing via national partner" with a **Wisetack pay-over-time link** (flat 3.9% per-transaction fee, no setup/subscription cost or monthly minimums per Wisetack's merchant page), and replace the national "find a location" page with **individual town landing pages** that turn Jeremiah Ugbine's local presence into the authority story franchises can't tell.
- Keep the high-converting one-page landing page as the **conversion hub**, but spin out 6–8 standalone SEO/objection-handling pages (FAQ, vs-replacement, process, gallery, town pages, About/founder story, warranty, and a "is refinishing right for me?" quiz) that each route back into the existing multi-step lead form.

## Key Findings

**1. A standalone FAQ page is the most universal pattern and Jerry & Co.'s clearest miss.** N-Hance, Spray-Net, and Miracle Method all run extensive FAQ pages organized into topic clusters. N-Hance groups FAQs into "Questions about Cabinet Refinishing," "Questions about Floor Refinishing," and "Questions about N-Hance." Spray-Net's FAQ explicitly tackles the hardest objections: "Can you paint my cabinets if the laminate/melamine is starting to come off?", "Is your coating scratch resistant? My cabinets get handled quite a lot," "Can I be in the house with my pets/children while you paint?", and "What if I have some water damage on the cabinets under the sink?" Miracle Method's FAQs are organized by surface and cover durability ("How durable is the finish?"), longevity ("up to 15-20 years or more"), cost savings ("up to 75% over the cost of replacement"), and care instructions. Kitchen Tune-Up notably does NOT have a standalone consumer FAQ page — it embeds FAQs on its homepage instead. Surface Experts has no FAQ page at all (it is a B2B/commercial-facing site).

**2. The "refinishing vs. replacement" comparison page is a content category unto itself.** N-Hance runs multiple dedicated URLs on this single theme — "Cabinet Refinishing vs. Replacement: A Professional's Perspective," "Refinish vs. Replace? What's Best for Your Cabinets," and "Benefits of Refinishing vs. Replacing Cabinets." The consistent framing: refinishing saves "50% to 75% of the cost compared to cabinet replacement"; replacement runs "$6,000 to over $30,000"; and older cabinets are "higher quality than those produced today," so refinishing preserves solid hardwood instead of swapping it for "less sturdy, mass-produced options." Miracle Method's whole brand rests on "Don't replace — renew!" and Surface Experts' on "Repair, Don't Replace." This is exactly Jerry & Co.'s positioning against "full remodel GCs" and deserves its own page.

**3. Every consumer-facing franchise has an interactive visualizer, positioned prominently in the nav.** Kitchen Tune-Up has an "AI Design Tool" (`/design-tool/`) as a top-level nav item — upload a photo of your kitchen, explore cabinet colors/door styles/finishes in minutes; it's positioned as a low-commitment first step that "enhances collaboration during consultations." CertaPro's "My PaintColors" visualizer lets users upload a home photo and preview colors; N-Hance has a "Design Tool" in its top nav; Spray-Net has a "Visualizer / Try it!" link persistent across its entire nav. These tools are explicitly framed as inspiration ("for inspiration only, not installation guides"), and feed users into consultations.

**4. The "how it works"/process page is standard and detailed.** Spray-Net's "How It Works" page walks through the in-home spray-booth setup, masking, degreasing/sanding, exclusive grain filler, high-build primer, and the water-based 2-component polyurethane topcoat. Jerry & Co. already has a 5-step process explainer on the homepage — but competitors give it a dedicated, indexable URL. Jerry & Co. uses the same 2K polyurethane technology Spray-Net markets heavily (Spray-Net benchmarks its "2-component polyurethane kitchen cabinet paint" against "ASTM and Kitchen Cabinet Manufacturers' Association standards" and claims it "outperforms all leading kitchen cabinet paints for durability in every category"), which is a strong differentiator that warrants its own page.

**5. Warranty presentation varies widely — and reveals the solo-operator opportunity.** Spray-Net leads with a "15-year, no-peel warranty" that is transferable to subsequent owners and covers "labor and the cost of the products." N-Hance offers a "two (2) year" warranty against "flaking, peeling, or product discoloration." Miracle Method offers a "five-year warranty" and explicitly contrasts it with manufacturers: "While new bathtub manufacturers, like Kohler and American Standard, offer only one year warranties... each individual Miracle Method franchise provides a five year warranty." Critically, even the franchises admit the warranty is honored by "each individual franchise" — meaning the warranty's real value is local accountability, which a solo owner-operator can match or beat.

**6. Lead capture is consistently a guided, multi-step "get an estimate" flow, not a bare form.** N-Hance's estimate flow is framed as a 4-step narrative: "fill out the form → a local N-Hance expert will call or text you → we'll visit your home and take measurements → get your free quote, no pressure, no obligation → book when ready." Spray-Net uses an algorithmic on-tablet quote ("The Spray-Network") that promises "no surprises... no arbitrary pricing." Research on form design strongly supports the multi-step approach: the canonical Venture Harbour case study reports its own enquiry form rose "from 0.96% to 8.1%" conversion — a 743% increase — per founder/CEO Marcus Taylor, who also documents lifts of "35% for BrokerNotes... and 214% for an astroturf company" (treat as directional; the cited Leadformly tool has since been discontinued). The mechanism is well-established: starting with an easy, non-personal question (project type) before asking for name/phone increases completion. Jerry & Co.'s existing 9-field discovery questionnaire is on the right track but should be explicitly multi-step with a progress bar.

**7. "Find a location" is the one franchise pattern that does NOT transfer — and that's an advantage.** N-Hance (255 franchised U.S. locations across 39 states as of its 2025 Franchise Disclosure Document, per FranchiseGrade — well below the brand's promotional "nearly 500" claims), Miracle Method ("over 140 locations"), Surface Experts, and Kitchen Tune-Up all run national location-finder pages because they must route visitors to independently-owned franchises. A solo operator has no such routing problem. The local equivalent is per-town service-area pages (Medford, Malden, Everett, Arlington, Somerville, Cambridge, Woburn, Winchester), each with unique content, local project photos, and town-specific testimonials.

**8. About/brand-story pages humanize the franchises weakly — Jerry & Co. can win here decisively.** Surface Experts' About page reads as a company/franchise-team story (origin "in the Pacific Northwest in 2018," now "28 states and counting," profiling franchising leadership). Kitchen Tune-Up splits its story across a "Why Us" page and a separate "History" page. None can credibly claim a single, named, local craftsman. Jerry & Co.'s owner-operator story (Jeremiah Ugbine, 7 years in Greater Boston, MA HIC #208336, EPA RRP lead-safe certified) is the strongest trust asset in the entire competitive set and is currently underused.

### B) SPECIFIC NEW PAGE RECOMMENDATIONS (priority-ordered)

**1. "Cabinet Refinishing vs. Replacement (and vs. Cheap Painters)" — `/cabinet-refinishing-vs-replacement/`** *(Highest priority)*
This is the single most valuable page to add. Frame Jerry & Co.'s three-way position explicitly:
- **vs. full remodel/replacement:** "$1,800–$9,500 vs. $20,000–$50,000+ for replacement" — save 50–75%, keep your layout, done in 2–5 days not weeks, and your existing solid-wood boxes are often higher quality than today's mass-produced replacements.
- **vs. cheap painters:** factory-grade 2K (2-component) polyurethane spray finish — not brushed latex that chips. Reference that this is the same chemistry class that meets Kitchen Cabinet Manufacturers' Association durability standards.
- Include a decision table ("Refinish if… / Replace if…") modeled on N-Hance's, plus a cost-comparison graphic and a CTA into the lead form.

**2. Dedicated FAQ page — `/faq/`** *(High priority)*
Organize into four accordion clusters with FAQPage JSON-LD schema (note: as of May 7, 2026, Google no longer shows FAQ rich-result snippets in Search — see Caveats — so the value is now content depth, AI-Overview/voice-search eligibility, and on-page objection handling rather than SERP accordions). Suggested Q&As drawn directly from competitor objection patterns:
- *Durability & Warranty:* "How long does a 2K polyurethane finish last?"; "Will it chip or peel?"; "What's your warranty?"; "What happens if I scratch it?" (offer touch-up).
- *Material compatibility:* "Can you refinish laminate/melamine/thermofoil cabinets, or only wood?"; "What if the laminate is peeling?"; "Will the wood grain still show?"; "Can you change the color completely (e.g., oak to white)?"
- *Process & disruption:* "Is it messy? Do you sand?"; "What are the fumes/VOCs like — can I stay home with kids and pets?"; "How long am I without my kitchen?"; "Do you work on-site or take doors away?"
- *Cost & value:* "How much does it cost?"; "Is it cheaper than replacement/refacing?"; "Does it add home value?"; "Do you offer financing?"

**3. About / Founder Story — `/about/`** *(High priority — biggest differentiator)*
Lead with Jeremiah Ugbine as a named, local, owner-operator craftsman: 7 years serving Greater Boston, personally on every job (no subcontractors, no rotating crews), MA HIC #208336, EPA RRP Lead-Safe certified. Explicitly contrast with the franchise model: "When you hire Jerry & Co., you get the owner — not a franchisee's subcontractor." Include a photo, a "why I do this" narrative, and the trust credentials.

**4. Per-town service-area pages — `/cabinet-refinishing-{town}/`** *(High SEO priority)*
One unique page each for Medford, Malden, Everett, Arlington, Somerville, Cambridge, Woburn, Winchester. Each must have ≥80–100% unique content (local project photos, town-specific testimonials, references to local neighborhoods/housing stock such as the area's prevalence of older homes with solid-wood cabinets) to avoid Google's "doorway page" penalty. Display business name + phone (no fake address — Jerry & Co. is a service-area business). Add ServiceArea + LocalBusiness schema.

**5. "Is Refinishing Right for My Cabinets?" interactive quiz — `/cabinet-quiz/`** *(Medium priority — differentiator)*
A 4–6 question quiz (cabinet material? structurally sound? happy with layout? want color change? budget range?) that ends with a tailored recommendation (refinish / refresh / suggest replacement referral) AND routes into the lead form with answers pre-populated. This is the solo-operator equivalent of the franchise visualizers — cheaper to build, higher-intent, and doubles as a qualification tool.

**6. Finish & Color Visualizer / Color Gallery — `/colors/`** *(Medium priority)*
A full visualizer is expensive to build. The realistic solo-operator version: a curated color gallery (designer-picked palettes shown on real refinished cabinets) plus, if budget allows, an embedded third-party visualizer widget. Position as "explore finishes" in the nav.

**7. Warranty/Guarantee page — `/guarantee/`** *(Medium priority)*
See solo-operator warranty strategy below.

**8. Financing page — `/financing/`** *(Medium priority)*
See solo-operator financing strategy below.

### C) STRATEGIC SITEMAP / WIREFRAME — standalone pages vs. anchors

```
HOMEPAGE (existing one-page landing — stays the CONVERSION HUB)
│  Hero + before/after slider  → anchor #top
│  Trust bar                    → anchor
│  Services grid (4 cards)      → anchor #services  (each card links OUT to a service/standalone page)
│  5-step process               → anchor #process   (links OUT to /our-process/)
│  Before/after gallery          → anchor #gallery   (links OUT to full /gallery/)
│  Service-area chips            → anchor            (each chip links OUT to /cabinet-refinishing-{town}/)
│  Multi-step lead form          → anchor #quote     ★ THE CONVERSION TARGET ★
│  Good/Better/Best pricing      → anchor #pricing
│  Footer                        → links to ALL standalone pages
│
└── STANDALONE PAGES (each ends with a CTA → homepage #quote form):
     ├─ /cabinet-refinishing-vs-replacement/   ←─┐
     ├─ /faq/                                    │  all interlink to
     ├─ /about/  (founder story)                 │  each other via
     ├─ /our-process/                            │  contextual links
     ├─ /guarantee/                              │  + footer
     ├─ /financing/                              │
     ├─ /colors/  (visualizer/color gallery)     │
     ├─ /cabinet-quiz/  → routes INTO #quote ─────┤
     └─ /cabinet-refinishing-{town}/ ×8  ─────────┘
```

**Rule of thumb applied:** Anything that (a) earns search traffic, (b) handles a deep objection, or (c) needs its own indexable URL becomes a **standalone page**. Anything that is purely a conversion-moment or visual proof stays an **anchor section** on the one-pager. The one-page form remains the single conversion endpoint; every standalone page is a spoke that drives back to it. Navigation should be lean (Services, Why Refinish, Gallery, FAQ, About, Service Areas, + a persistent "Get a Free Quote" button) with secondary pages (guarantee, financing, process, colors, quiz) in sub-nav/footer per contractor-site best practice.

### D) CONTAINER-BY-CONTAINER MODIFICATIONS to the existing 9 sections

**Container 1 — Hero + before/after slider:** Add one objection-pre-empting sub-headline beneath the positioning statement: *"Factory-grade 2K polyurethane finish — won't chip or peel like brushed-on paint. Most kitchens done in 2–5 days, not weeks."* Keep it tight; link the phrase "won't chip or peel" OUT to `/guarantee/`. Do NOT add a wall of copy here.

**Container 2 — Trust bar (HIC #208336, EPA RRP, insured):** Add a fourth micro-element: a written-guarantee badge ("Workmanship Guarantee — see details") linking to `/guarantee/`, and make "EPA RRP Lead-Safe" link to a short explainer (matters for the older housing stock common across these ICC towns). This is microcontent, not a paragraph.

**Container 3 — 4-card services grid:** Under the featured cabinet-refinishing card, add a single objection-handling line: *"Factory finish for a fraction of replacement cost — typically 50–75% less than new cabinets."* Each card's "learn more" should link OUT to its standalone/service page rather than expanding inline. Keep the bathroom/painting cards' microcopy parallel.

**Container 4 — 5-step process explainer:** Add a one-line reassurance under the step that involves prep/spraying: *"On-site, low-VOC, low-odor process with contained dust — safe to stay home with kids and pets."* (This directly answers the disruption/mess objection competitors address — Spray-Net explicitly markets its ventilated in-home spray booth so "both dust and the minimal paint fumes... filter straight outdoors.") Add a "See our full process →" link OUT to `/our-process/`. Keep the on-page version to 5 concise steps.

**Container 5 — Filterable before/after gallery:** Keep on homepage but add a "View full gallery →" link to `/gallery/`. Add one caption-level durability note on a hero example: *"2K polyurethane finish — 18 months later, no chips."* (real, dated proof beats claims). Tag gallery images with town names where possible to support the service-area pages.

**Container 6 — Service-area chips:** Convert each chip from a passive label into a LINK to that town's `/cabinet-refinishing-{town}/` page. Add a single line: *"Locally owned and operated — serving [primary towns] for 7 years."* This is the solo-operator answer to the franchise "find a location" page.

**Container 7 — Project discovery questionnaire/lead form (THE conversion target):** Restructure the existing 9 fields into an explicit **multi-step flow with a progress bar**. Step 1 = project type (single click, no personal info) → Step 2 = town + timeline + size → Step 3 = color/notes → Step 4 = name/phone/email LAST. Add a reassurance line near the submit: *"No-pressure, free in-home estimate. You'll get a detailed written quote — most within [X] days."* Mirror N-Hance's "what happens next" 4-step explainer right beside the form so the user knows the process. Make the `/cabinet-quiz/` results feed directly into this form pre-filled. (Use 3–4 steps; conversion research shows lift comes from this structure but should be validated against Jerry & Co.'s own data — see Caveats.)

**Container 8 — Good/Better/Best pricing preview:** Add objection-handling microcopy under the tiers: a "vs. replacement" anchor line (*"Even our Best tier is a fraction of the $20k–$50k+ cost of new cabinets"*) linking to `/cabinet-refinishing-vs-replacement/`, and a financing line (*"Pay over time from ~$X/mo — check your rate in minutes, no impact to credit"*) linking to `/financing/`. Add the workmanship-guarantee line here too, since price objection and risk objection co-occur at the pricing moment.

**Container 9 — Footer:** Expand into the site's navigational backbone: link every standalone page (FAQ, vs-replacement, process, about, guarantee, financing, colors, quiz, all 8 town pages), repeat NAP (name/phone, service area — no fake address), HIC #208336, EPA RRP cert, and a final "Get a Free Quote" CTA. Add LocalBusiness schema in the footer template.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Build now (highest ROI, lowest effort):**
1. Add the **`/cabinet-refinishing-vs-replacement/`** page and the **`/faq/`** page (with FAQPage schema). These capture the highest-intent searches and kill the biggest objections. Restructure the homepage **lead form into a multi-step flow** — this is a conversion win independent of any new page.
2. Add the **`/about/` founder-story page** — it's nearly free to produce and is Jerry & Co.'s single strongest differentiator against every franchise.
3. Apply the **Container 1–9 microcopy edits** above (durability, disruption, vs-replacement, guarantee lines).

**Stage 2 — Build next (SEO compounding):**
4. Ship the **8 per-town service-area pages** with genuinely unique local content and the `/our-process/`, `/gallery/`, `/guarantee/`, and `/financing/` standalone pages.
5. Stand up **Wisetack** financing and publish the guarantee language.

**Stage 3 — Differentiate:**
6. Build the **`/cabinet-quiz/`** (routes into the lead form) and the **`/colors/`** visualizer/gallery.

**Benchmarks that change the plan:**
- If the multi-step form's completion rate doesn't beat the current single form within ~1,000 sessions or 2 weeks, A/B test field order and step count (3–4 steps is optimal).
- If town pages don't rank within ~3–4 months, audit for thin/duplicate content (the #1 cause of service-area-page failure) and add more unique local proof.
- If financed jobs stay rare, drop to a simple payment-calculator link rather than paying for any subscription product.
- Track which FAQ questions get opened most; promote the top 3 objections into homepage microcopy.

## Caveats
- **FAQ rich results are deprecated.** Per Google's FAQPage developer documentation: "As of May 7, 2026, FAQ rich results are no longer appearing in Google Search. We will be dropping the FAQ search appearance, rich result report, and support in the Rich results test in June 2026... support for the FAQ rich result in the Search Console API will be removed in August 2026." Per Search Engine Journal, this "appears to end eligibility for the remaining FAQ rich results for government and health sites, too." FAQPage remains a valid Schema.org type Google still parses — so build the FAQ page for content depth, voice/AI-Overview eligibility, and on-page objection handling, not for SERP accordion snippets.
- **Form-conversion statistics are vendor-published.** The widely-cited multi-step-form lift figures come from form-software vendors and case studies (Venture Harbour's own form rose "from 0.96% to 8.1%," a 743% increase per founder Marcus Taylor; Formstack/HubSpot report similar directional lifts). The cited Leadformly tool has since been discontinued. Treat all such figures as directional and validate with Jerry & Co.'s own A/B test.
- **Competitor location and warranty claims are marketing claims.** Brand "500+ locations" figures inflate audited counts (N-Hance's 2025 FDD shows 255 U.S. franchised locations across 39 states). Independent reviews of N-Hance also show real-world peeling/chipping complaints despite the marketing — underscoring that for a solo operator, *demonstrated* local accountability and dated before/after proof will out-convert any warranty number.
- **The founder's specific background details** (exact years, certification specifics) should be verified against Jerry & Co.'s records before publishing; this report uses the brief's stated facts (Jeremiah Ugbine, 7 years, HIC #208336, EPA RRP).
- **I was unable to independently verify Jerry & Co.'s business details on the public web**; all client-specific facts are taken from the task brief.
